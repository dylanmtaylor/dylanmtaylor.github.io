Great News: Gmail Gets Free Voice Calling to US and Canada

25 Aug 2010

[caption id="" align="alignleft" width="143" caption="Image via Wikipedia"]<img class=" " title="Gmail's logo" src="http://www.dylanmtaylor.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/Gmail_logo.png" alt="Gmail's logo" width="143" height="59" /></a>[/caption]

</div>
Google</a> just added a really cool feature to their already great Gmail</a> service... free voice calling</a>. It is now possible to simple click a link (labeled "Call phone"), type in a phone number</a>, and dial it, using your computer's speakers and microphone. The only downside is that you have to install a proprietary plugin</a>, which is available for Microsoft Windows</a>, Mac OS X</a>, and Linux</a>. I tested the service on my computer running Ubuntu Linux</a> 10.10 (Maverick Meerkat</a>) Alpha 3</a> (64-bit edition). The call quality is excellent, and it rivals other competing VoIP</a> solutions such as Skype</a>, not to mention, it costs absolutely nothing</em>, as long as you are dialing a number located in the United States</a> and Canada</a>. This is a really great addition to Gmail, and I hope to see Google add the ability to dial numbers from their Google Voice service too. Another thing that I'd really</em> love to see as well is the ability to dial phone numbers using your Google Voice number from the Google Voice application for Android and other mobile phone</a> platforms. Also, if you are calling number outside of the United States or Canada, you can also make international calls at competitive rates. Check it out!
